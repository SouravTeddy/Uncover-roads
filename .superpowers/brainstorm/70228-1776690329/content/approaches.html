3 Approaches: Conversational Origin Card

Replacing the Hotel / Airport / Drop pin chips with something that feels alive

A

Progressive Reveal

Card opens with a single warm question: "Where are you starting your trip from?" — then presents 3 friendly answer tiles (Hotel, Airport, I haven't decided yet). Each answer reveals a follow-up inline — hotel → check-in time picker, airport → landing time, undecided → a "find a place" panel with Google Hotels / Booking.com shortcuts.

Pros

  • Feels like a conversation, not a form
  • Reveals complexity only when needed
  • Minimal taps for decided users

Cons

  • Slightly longer flow for hotel users (vs. current)
B

Bubble Chat Thread

The card behaves like a mini chat — assistant sends a message bubble, user taps a reply chip. "Hey! Where will you kick off your trip?" → reply chips: Hotel · Airport · Not sure yet. Answer reveals the next message bubble with a follow-up. Mirrors the existing JourneyAdvisorThread pattern already in the app.

Pros

  • Most "conversational" feel
  • Consistent with JourneyAdvisorThread
  • Memorable, brand-differentiating

Cons

  • More visual space per step
  • Can feel slow if user knows what they want
C

Smart Single Screen

One screen, no steps. A bold heading "Where are you starting from?" with 3 large tap targets below. The "Not sure yet" tile is equal weight — not hidden or secondary. Tapping hotel/airport opens an inline search right on the same card. Undecided opens a bottom sheet with booking links.

Pros

  • Fastest path for all user types
  • No perceived "steps"
  • Clean, modern feel

Cons

  • Less narrative/conversational
  • Check-in time needs a separate tap

My recommendation

A (Progressive Reveal) — gives the most natural conversation feel without the visual overhead of chat bubbles. It's the right balance between "interesting" and "fast." The "not sure yet" path becomes a first-class citizen, not an afterthought. And it sets up the future neighborhood suggestions (option C you mentioned) as a natural expansion of that undecided panel.
