Issue 3 — Smarter Itinerary Display

Two approaches to making the itinerary feel intelligent and aware

A

Enriched stops + gap cards

Each stop shows the AI's why (e.g. "Chosen for your love of history — best visited before crowds"). Detected gaps in the schedule (no lunch stop between 12–2pm, no dinner stop by 7pm) are surfaced as inline suggestion cards: "No restaurant in your lunch window — add one?" with a shortcut back to the map.

B

Summary header + inline gap nudges

A persona-aware summary banner at the top ("Built for your Curator style — 4 cultural stops, 1 lunch break"). Missing meal windows show a subtle nudge row inline in the timeline. Less verbose per-stop, more overview-focused.

Weather animation — always shown on the itinerary screen

W1

Animated background gradient

The header area shifts color + motion based on weather — sunny → warm amber pulse, rainy → cool blue falling drops, cloudy → slow grey drift. Subtle and performant.

W2

Canvas particle animation

Rain drops, snow flakes, or sun rays drawn on a canvas overlay. More visually rich but heavier. Works best when weather is dramatic.
